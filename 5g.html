How does 5G work?

Radio waves or radio frequency energy is used to transmit and receive data (wireless communication) between communities. 5G is designed to use higher radio frequencies that are neater and more capable of transferring more information in a shorter time period.

The special construction of 5G leads to several favorable properties, such as: very low latency (response time is predicted to be around 1ms, while for 4G, it is 40-50ms), greater capacity and reliability. However, the waves are not suitable for long-distance-travel, as they can be easily blocked by many physical objects, such as: walls and trees. So that 5G applies multiple input and multiple output (MIMO) antennae in order to improve the signals and capacity. Instead of singular masts, smaller transmitter will be installed.
